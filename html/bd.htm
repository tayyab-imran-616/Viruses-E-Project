BLACK DEATH

"Historic plague that devastated medieval populations.
Mass fatalities across Europe and Asia.""

VIRUS INTRODUCTION

The Black Death began in the 14th century, likely originating in Central Asia before spreading across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It killed an estimated 75-200 million people between 1347 and 1351, making it one of the deadliest pandemics in history.

The disease was caused by the bacterium Yersinia pestis, transmitted by fleas on rats. At that time, no vaccine existed, and medical knowledge was very limited.

BLACK DEATH: CONTAINMENT PROTOCOLS

Sanitary measures established during the 1347 Great Plague

Isolation of patients

Anyone showing plague symptoms should be kept separate from healthy people to prevent spreading the disease.
