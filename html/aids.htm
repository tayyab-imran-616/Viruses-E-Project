VIRUS INTRODUCTION

HIV is believed to have originated in Central Africa in the early 20th century and was officially recognized in 1981. It has spread globally, affecting over 85 million people, with more than 40 million deaths recorded. HIV attacks the immune system and can lead to AIDS if untreated.

There is no complete cure, but antiretroviral therapy (ART), developed in the 1990s, allows people to live long and healthy lives.

PRIMARY SYMPTOMS

Common Signs and Symptoms.

Extreme Weight Loss

Rapid and unexplained loss of body weight.

Persistent Fever

Long-lasting fever without clear cause.

Night Sweats

Heavy sweating during sleep.

Severe Fatigue

Constant tiredness due to weakened immunity.
