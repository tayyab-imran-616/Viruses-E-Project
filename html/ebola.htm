VIRUS INTRODUCTION

Ebola was first identified in 1976 near the Ebola River in the Democratic Republic of Congo. Since then, outbreaks have occurred mainly in African countries, infecting thousands and causing over 11,000 deaths during the largest outbreak (2014-2016).

The virus spreads through direct contact with infected body fluids and has a high fatality rate. The first approved Ebola vaccine was introduced in 2019 to control outbreaks.

PRIMARY SYMPTOMS

Common Signs and Symptoms.

High Fever

Sudden and intense rise in body temperature.

Severe Headache

Strong and persistent head pain.

Muscle Pain

Body aches and joint discomfort.

Bleeding

Internal or external bleeding in serious cases.
