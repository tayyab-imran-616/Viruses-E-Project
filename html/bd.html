PATHOGEN PROFILE

Yersinia Pestis Archive

The Black Death was a devastating global pandemic of bubonic plague that struck Europe and Asia in the mid-1300s. It was caused by the bacterium Yersinia pestis, transmitted through fleas on migratory rodents.

The "Vertex System" reconstructs the 14th-century transmission nodes, providing deep-dive analytics into plague-doctor protocols and systemic societal collapse.

PRIMARY SYMPTOMS

Archival indicators of Yersinia Pestis infiltration.

Buboes

Painful, swollen lymph nodes in the groin, armpit, or neck areas.

High Fever

Sudden onset of high temperature, chills, and systemic weakness.

Necrosis

Blackening and death of skin and tissues on extremities (fingers/toes).

Septicemia

Rapid spread of the pathogen through the bloodstream causing organ failure.
