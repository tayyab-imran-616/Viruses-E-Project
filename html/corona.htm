VIRUS INTRODUCTION

Covid-19 emerged in late 2019 in Wuhan, China, and quickly spread worldwide. It affected over 200 countries, infecting more than 700 million people and causing over 6 million confirmed deaths globally.

The first vaccines were developed in 2020, helping reduce severe cases and fatalities. The virus spreads mainly through respiratory droplets and continues to evolve with new variants.

PRIMARY SYMPTOMS

Common Signs and Symptoms.

High Fever

Elevated body temperature, common early sign of infection.

Dry Cough

Persistent cough without mucus production.

Fatigue

Ongoing tiredness and low energy levels.

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing in moderate to severe cases.

SAFETY PROTOCOLS

Execute the following directives to minimize virus infiltration.

01

Mask Usage in Public Areas

Wear face masks in crowded or indoor spaces to reduce airborne transmission.

02

Vaccination & Boosters

Stay updated with recommended vaccine doses to lower severe infection risk.

03

Hygiene & Distancing Measures

Maintain hand hygiene and safe physical distance to limit virus spread.
